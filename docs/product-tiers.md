## Tier Distribution Rationale

- **Good (40%)**: High volume of low-cost items creates accessible entry points. Straws, toothbrushes, and totes are typical first purchases for eco-curious customers. Low shipping weight keeps fulfillment costs down.
- **Better (43%)**: The catalog's center of gravity. Bottles, tumblers, wraps, and bags are the categories with the highest repeat purchase rates and gift potential. These are the products that build long-term customer relationships.
- **Best (17%)**: A deliberate minority. Premium kits serve as aspirational anchors and gift purchases. The safety razor line in particular drives high lifetime value through blade refills (post-launch opportunity).

## Margin Targets Summary

| Tier | Target Gross Margin | Rationale |
|------|-------------------|-----------|
| Good | 55–65% | Low per-unit COGS (bamboo, wheat straw, cotton); high volume expected |
| Better | 50–60% | Moderate COGS (stainless steel, silicone, insulation); strong AOV |
| Best | 45–55% | Higher COGS (multi-component kits, premium finishes); higher absolute margin per sale |

**Blended target**: ~55% gross margin across the catalog, assuming tier mix of approximately 50% Good, 35% Better, 15% Best by unit volume.
