# Rheuse — Tone of Voice

> **Status**: Active — Last updated 2026-03-19

## Brand Voice in One Line

Rheuse sounds like a knowledgeable friend who's genuinely enthusiastic about beautiful, practical things — and happens to know a lot about sustainable materials.

## Personality Traits

| Trait | What It Means | What It Doesn't Mean |
|-------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Warm** | Like recommending a product to a friend. Personal, approachable, real. | Overly familiar, sloppy, or trying to be everyone's best friend. |
| **Confident** | We know our materials, our craft, and our claims. We state things clearly. | Arrogant, aggressive, or dismissive of other choices. |
| **Honest** | Specific and verifiable — "BPA-free 18/8 stainless steel" not "eco-friendly." | Blunt, self-deprecating, or volunteering negatives unprompted. |
| **Aspirational** | Sustainability as an upgrade. Owning a Rheuse product should feel like a step up. | Guilt-tripping, lecturing, or implying the reader is part of a problem. |
| **Playful** | Light touches of wit. A wink, not a comedy routine. | Silly, kidsy, meme-driven, or trying too hard to be clever. |

## Writing Do's

- Lead with benefits, then materials — "Keeps coffee hot for 8 hours. Double-walled stainless steel."
- Use active voice and short sentences. Clear beats clever every time.
- Be specific — name the material, the measurement, the duration. Vague claims erode trust.
- Frame sustainability as an upgrade — you're gaining something, not giving something up.
- Write in second person ("you", "your") — talk to the customer, not at them.
- Let product quality do the convincing. Describe what it feels like to hold, use, and live with the product.
- Use contractions naturally — "it's," "you'll," "we're" — unless the tone requires more weight.

## Writing Don'ts

- Don't use guilt or shame — "you're destroying the planet" has no place here.
- Don't make unverifiable claims — "saves the ocean" is not ours to promise.
- Don't use buzzwords without substance — "sustainable," "green," "eco-friendly" mean nothing alone. Always say *why*.
- Don't preach — we're a shop, not a sermon.
- Don't use ALL CAPS or stack exclamation marks.
- Don't sacrifice clarity for cleverness. If the reader has to decode the sentence, rewrite it.
- Don't disparage competitors or conventional products — just make ours irresistible.

## Sample Copy

### Product Card — Good ✅
> **Bamboo Utensil Roll** — Fork, knife, spoon, and chopsticks in a compact cotton roll. Toss it in your bag, skip the plastic drawer at work. Naturally antibacterial bamboo, hand-finished.

### Product Card — Bad ❌
> ~~**Eco-Friendly Bamboo Cutlery Kit™** — Do your part for the environment! Every year 40 billion plastic utensils end up in landfills. Be the change! 🌍~~

### Product Description Excerpt — Good ✅
> The double-walled vacuum keeps your coffee at the temperature you made it — for up to 8 hours hot, 12 cold. The ceramic interior means no metallic taste, just whatever you brewed this morning. Fits standard cup holders, dishwasher-safe lid, matte Forest finish that won't show fingerprints.

### Homepage Hero — Good ✅
> **Beautiful things, used again and again.**
> Premium reusables designed for real life — not the recycling bin.

### CTA Buttons
- ✅ "Add to bag" / "Build your kit" / "Shop bottles" / "See the collection"
- ❌ "Buy now!!" / "Save the planet" / "Don't miss out!!!" / "Go green today"

### Email Subject Lines
- ✅ "Your Rheuse order is on its way 📦"
- ✅ "Meet your new morning routine"
- ✅ "The utensil set that replaced our junk drawer"
- ❌ "🚨 LAST CHANCE to save the earth!!!"
- ❌ "You NEED to stop using plastic NOW"

### 404 Page
> **This page wandered off.** Maybe it's composting somewhere. Let's get you back to [the good stuff](/).

### Sold Out Notice
> **This one's popular.** Enter your email and we'll let you know the moment it's back.

## Language Toolkit

### Words We Use
| Instead of... | We say... |
|---------------|-----------|
| Eco-friendly | Specific material claim — "made from organic cotton," "BPA-free stainless steel" |
| Sustainable | "Built to last," "designed to be reused," "replaces ~X disposables per year" |
| Green | "Thoughtfully made," "low-waste" |
| Save the planet | "A better daily routine" |
| Buy | "Add to bag," "grab," "start with" |
| Cheap | "Accessible," "great value" |

### Eco Claims Format
Every sustainability claim follows this pattern: **specific material → specific benefit → verifiable fact.**

- ✅ "Organic cotton tote — machine-washable, rated to carry 25 lbs. Replaces roughly 500 disposable bags over its lifetime."
- ✅ "BPA-free borosilicate glass with a silicone sleeve. Dishwasher safe, oven safe to 450°F."
- ❌ "This eco-friendly product helps save the environment!"

## Voice by Context

| Context | Energy | Formality | Example |
|---------|--------|-----------|---------|
| Product descriptions | Medium-high | Conversational | "Lightweight, leak-proof, and fits in your coat pocket." |
| Homepage hero | High | Warm, direct | "Beautiful things, used again and again." |
| Email — transactional | Medium | Friendly, clear | "Your order has shipped. Here's your tracking link." |
| Email — marketing | Medium-high | Conversational | "We just restocked the Travel Cup in three new finishes." |
| Legal pages | Low | Professional | Standard legal language, plain English where possible. |
| Error messages | Low | Helpful, calm | "Something went wrong on our end. Try refreshing, or contact us." |
| Social media | High | Casual, playful | "Morning coffee tastes better when your cup has a story." |
| Category pages | Medium | Informative, warm | "Everything you need for lunch — nothing you'll throw away." |
